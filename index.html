Urban Agriculture Incentive Zones

Urban Agriculture Incentive Zones allow visionary property owners and project creators to continue the legacy of the Valley of the Heart’s Delight - our name before we were known as Silicon Valley - in San Jose and Santa Clara County. Vacant lots can become centers for the production, distribution, recycling, and vending of locally grown food, benefitting a multitude of neighborhoods through an increase in good food, public safety, and community spirit.

Under new legislation passed in 2016, private land owners in San Jose and in unincorporated areas of Santa Clara County can now receive a significant reduction in property taxes if they allow an undeveloped parcel to be used for urban agriculture. Examples of urban agriculture include community gardens, family farms, greenhouses, permaculture sites, and community supported agriculture. Sites can vend agricultural products as well.

1. Review your eligibility

Please refer to the checklist below to determine if your property qualifies for the tax incentive.
  • Owner Requirements
    • Property owners must agree to a minimum contract term of 5 years
    • Pay $308 per application (additional fees will be required for the contract with the County Clerk Recorder’s Office and the annual inspection).
  • Property Requirements
    • Eligible properties must:
      • Be between .1 and 1 acre in size
      • Be entirely devoted to agricultural activities
        • Dwelling units are not permitted, any buildings on site must be part of the agricultural use.
      • Be located in a zoning district where agriculture is permitted
      • Have proof of metered water service on site
